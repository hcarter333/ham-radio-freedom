NTSC Frame Rates and Lines

 

Fast scan, (NTSC), television sends video as a progression of still pictures, (frames), at a rate of 29.97, (approximately 30), frames per second. Each frame is divided into a total of 525 visible horizontal lines. To help avoid flicker, the even numbered lines are sent first followed by odd numbered lines of the picture. The sets of even and odd lines are called fields.

 

The blanking portion of the video signal turns off the cathode ray tube, (CRT), scanning beam while it is moving from right to left, or from bottom to top of the transmitted picture. This prevents it from retracing lines that have already been displayed.
