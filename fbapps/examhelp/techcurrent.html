More About It

The current passing through a circuit is what allows that circuit to do its job. For example, the current flowing through a light bulb is what makes it light. The more current that flows through the bulb, the brighter the light produced. Circuit elements resist the flow of current. The tendency of a circuit element, (like a resistor, or a light bulb), to resist current flow is called resistance and is measured in ohms.


Current is supplied by power supplies. Typically, power supplies are rated in volts, . Voltage is a measure of the electromotive force of the power supply.
