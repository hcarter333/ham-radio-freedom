Protecting Circuits From Current Overloads

(Fuses)

A component known as a fuse is used to protect circuits from current overloads. A fuse is typically placed in series between the power supply and the circuits to be protected as shown below:

When too much current flows through the fuse it breaks down and stops the flow of current into the circuits being protected. Simple fuses consist of small strips of wire overheat and melt, breaking the circuit, when too much current flows through them.
