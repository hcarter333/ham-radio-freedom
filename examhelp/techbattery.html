Batteries

The schematic symbol for a battery is shown above.  Batteries can serve as the power source for station equipment.  Some batteries are rechargeable.  

Carbon-zinc batteries, the standard off-the-shelf battery for portable electronics, are not rechargeable.  

Lead-acid batteries are the common type of batteries found in cars and trucks.  They are rechargeable using a battery charger, or in a pinch, by connecting the battery to a car’s battery and running the engine.  Care should be taken with this kind of rechargeable battery.  If they are charged to quickly they can overheat and give off flammable gas or explode.  They should be stored in a vented area, or explosive gas can collect.
