Vestigial Sideband Transmission

 

The following explanation is a bit lengthy, so, first, the information that will answer the exam questions.

 

Vestigial sideband, (VSB), reduces the bandwidth transmission and allows for easy-to-build decode circuitry.  Unlike SSB modulation, VSB only removes a portion of the duplicated sideband, not the entire sideband.  

 

Now for the reasons why.

 

By using single sideband transmission the duplicated information in an AM signal can be removed and the bandwidth of the transmitted signal is cut in half. An easy to build filter is used to remove the extraneous sideband.

 

In addition to removing the duplicated sideband, the filter also removes some of the lower frequency signals of the original modulating source. For human voice these missing low frequencies are OK because the range of human hearing doesn't reach to those low frequencies anyway.

 

 

Some of the signals used for television require a very low modulating frequency. In this case, a easy to build, cheap SSB filter would remove these important signals. Constructing a filter that would allow these signals to pass and still eliminate the carrier frequency and opposite sideband is very expensive. To eliminate the need for this expensive filter, vestigial sideband transmission is used.

 

In vestigial sideband transmission, a cheaper filter is shifted so that it includes both the carrier frequency and all the low frequency modulation signals from the transmitted sideband and the extraneous sideband as well. The edge of the filter falls across the low frequency signals, the carrier and the extraneous low frequency signals reducing their amplitude in comparison to the original signal. Because there are two copies of the low frequency information, the original signal strength for these can be roughly restored allowing reception of what looks like a single sideband with complete modulation frequency information.

 

 

Exam Questions

 

Which of the following is an advantage of using vestigial sideband for standard fast scan TV transmissions?

A. The vestigial sideband carries the audio information

B. The vestigial sideband contains chroma information

C. Vestigial sideband reduces bandwidth while allowing for simple video detector circuitry

D. Vestigial sideband provides high frequency emphasis to sharpen the picture

 

As mentioned above, most of the other sideband is not transmitted.  This reduces bandwidth.  The portion that is transmitted allows the use of an easier to build filter adn detector.

 

 

What is vestigial sideband modulation?

A. Amplitude modulation in which one complete sideband and a portion of the other sideband is transmitted

B. A type of modulation in which one sideband is inverted

C. Narrow-band FM transmission achieved by filtering one sideband from the audio before frequency modulating the carrier

D. Spread spectrum modulation achieved by applying FM modulation following single sideband amplitude modulation

 

The signal starts out as AM with both sidebands present.  Unlike SSB only a portion of one of the sidebands is removed.  This reduces bandwidth while still allowing easy to build detector circuits to be used.
