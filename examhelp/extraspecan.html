Spectrum Analyzer

A spectrum analyzer is an instrument used to see the frequency content of a signal.  Unlike an oscilloscope that displays the amplitude of a signal over time, a frequency analyzer displays amplitude on the vertical axis and frequency on the horizontal axis.  With this information, you can tell what frequencies, (both intentional and spurious), your transmitter is emitting and at what relative power levels.

A spectrum analyzer can be used to check for intermodulation distortion in a SSB transmitter by transmitting two tones that are not harmonically related and looking at the amplitude of the sum and difference frequencies.  A spectrum analyzer is used in applications where it is important to monitor the frequency content of a signal such as:

The degree of isolation between the input and output ports of a 2 meter duplexer

Whether a crystal is operating on its fundamental or overtone frequency

or the spectral, (frequency components), output of a transmitter.

When a spectrum analyzer is used to measure the spectral output of a transmitter, care should be taken to attenuate the output level of the transmitter to a low enough level for the inputs of the spectrum analyzer.
