Inductors

 

                                     

 

The schematic symbol for a variable inductor is shown on the left.  The schematic symbol for an inductor is shown on the right.

 

An inductor is an electrical component that is usually made from a coil of wire.  An electric current passing through the coil of wire forms a magnetic field.  The component stores energy in this magnetic field. The ability to store energy in a magnetic field is known as inductance. Inductance is measured in units of henrys.
